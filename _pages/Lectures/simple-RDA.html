Test for colinearity

Then, we can calculate Variance Inflation Factors (VIF) for each of the constraints (variables) from the environmental matrix. If we find an environmental variable with VIF>10, weâll know that this variable presents colinearity with another or other variables. In that case, we would have to delete the variable from our initial dataset and redo all the analysis. In our example, no variable is redundant with each other (all of them have VIF<10). Linear dependencies can be explored by computing the variablesâ variance inflation factors (VIF), which measure the proportion by which the variance of a regression coefficient is inflated in the presence of other explanatory variables.
VIFs above 20 indicate strong collinearity. Ideally, VIFs above 10 should be at least examined, and avoided if possible. VIFs can be computed in vegan after RDA or CCA (Bocard et al.Â page 175)

Ordination plots

Scaling 1 (distance biplot): Se prioriza que la distancia entre objetos en el grafico respete tanto como sea posible las distancias euclidias de la matriz original. Los angulos entre vectores (variables) pueden ser distorsionados.

Scaling 2 (correlation biplot): Se prioriza que los angulos entre vectores respeten la correlacion original entre variables. La distancia entre objetos en el grafico puede estar distorsionada.

En resumen, utilizaremos scaling 1 si nos interesa mas ver como se diferencian los objetos, y scaling 2 si nos interesa mas ver como se relacionan las distintas variables.

Glossary

Constrained: refers to the amount of variance explained by the explanatory variables.

Inertia: is another name for variation or variance in this case.

An R2 statistic can be derived simply as Constrained / Total.

Total: refers to total variance. Constrained + Unconstrained = Total

Unconstrained: refers to the residual variance.

VIF: is a diagnostic tool done post-estimation to check for multicollinearity. VIF is not a model-building technique.
